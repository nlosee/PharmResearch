# Pharma Regulatory Pulse — 2026-05-11

## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Issues Class-Wide Testosterone Labeling Changes Following TRAVERSE and ABPM Data**
FDA informed sponsors of testosterone products of class-wide labeling changes after review of the TRAVERSE clinical trial and required postmarket ambulatory blood pressure monitoring studies. The agency also stated it is encouraging holders of approved testosterone NDA products to contact FDA regarding a supplemental NDA for a potential new indication in men with low libido associated with idiopathic hypogonadism. [Source 15][Source 20]
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-alerts-and-statements/fda-issues-class-wide-labeling-changes-testosterone-products
https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-takes-step-forward-testosterone-therapy-men

## Renal and Liver Disease
### Clinical Trials
**FDA Review Supports Accelerated Approval for IgAN Therapy**
FDA’s integrated review for VOYXACT (sibeprenlimab-szsi) in IgA nephropathy states that a substantial reduction in proteinuria is accepted as a reasonably likely surrogate endpoint for disease progression and that the benefits outweigh the risks. The review team recommends accelerated approval under Subpart E, with verification of clinical benefit to be demonstrated by a decrease in the rate of change in eGFR over a 100-week period. [Source 19]
https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2025/761434Orig1s000IntegratedR.pdf

**FDA Kidney Trial Guidance Highlights Nephrotoxicity Risk Assessment**
FDA’s kidney clinical trials guidance states that study eligibility criteria and clinical trial monitoring should be determined by compound nephrotoxicity risk, development phase, and population-related risk factors, including concomitant therapy, underlying disease, and comorbid conditions. The guidance also notes the need to assess patients for signs and symptoms of illness, including AKI, and to review laboratory values within 48 hours of abnormal test results. [Source 35]
https://www.fda.gov/media/175493/download

## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**EMA and FDA-Aligned AMR Framework Continues to Emphasize PK/PD and Benefit-Risk Evidence**
EMA’s antimicrobial resistance materials describe a joint effort with FDA and PMDA to align data requirements for antibacterial development, including guidance on pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic analyses and on demonstrating benefit-risk balance in children. The same materials note that the revision is intended to help developers design trials that meet multiple regulatory agencies’ requirements. [Source 23]
https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/human-regulatory-overview/public-health-threats/antimicrobial-resistance/antimicrobial-resistance-human-medicine

**EMA Maintains Gonorrhoea Development Focus Within AMR Policy**
EMA’s AMR materials also reference a new draft guideline on antibacterials that adds recommendations for studies in uncomplicated gonorrhoea, alongside a recommendation to seek EU Scientific Advice for development programs. The source frames this within broader efforts to support development of new antibiotics for human use. [Source 21][Source 23]
https://www.fda.gov/media/148232/download

## Cardiovascular Conditions
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Testosterone Labeling Action Incorporates Cardiovascular Safety Findings**
FDA’s testosterone labeling changes were driven in part by the TRAVERSE trial, which the agency reviewed alongside required ambulatory blood pressure monitoring studies. FDA stated that the ABPM studies confirmed an increase in blood pressure with use of all testosterone products, class-wide. [Source 15]
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-alerts-and-statements/fda-issues-class-wide-labeling-changes-testosterone-products

**AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure Guideline Reiterates HFpEF Management Recommendations**
The AHA/ACC/HFSA heart failure guideline states that patients with HFpEF and hypertension should have medication titrated to blood pressure targets in accordance with published clinical practice guidelines. It also states that in HFpEF, SGLT2 inhibitors can be beneficial in decreasing HF hospitalizations and cardiovascular mortality. [Source 7]
https://professional.heart.org/en/science-news/-/media/832EA0F4E73948848612F228F7FA2D35.ashx

### Clinical Trials
**FDA Guidance Supports COPD Drug Development Using SGRQ as a PRO Tool**
FDA’s COPD guidance addresses the use of the St. George’s Respiratory Questionnaire as a patient-reported outcome assessment tool in interventional clinical trials for COPD. The guidance is intended to inform sponsors developing drugs for COPD. [Source 12]

EMA states that its COPD guideline is a revision of CPMP/EWP562/98 and is intended to update prior guidance with new scientific knowledge and revise requirements for clinical investigation of medicinal products for COPD. The current effective version focuses on maintenance treatment of COPD. [Source 24]

## Special Populations (Weight Loss, Transplants, Pediatric)
### Clinical Trials
**FDA Weight Reduction Guidance Sets Pediatric Enrollment Expectations**
FDA’s draft weight reduction guidance states that pediatric trials should include subjects aged 6 years and older, with separate adolescent and younger pediatric cohorts generally recommended. It also recommends representative enrollment and substantial inclusion of subjects with severe obesity. [Source 2]
https://www.fda.gov/media/71252/download

**ClinicalTrials.gov Study Evaluates Exercise Plus Tirzepatide for Weight and Health Outcomes**
A ClinicalTrials.gov record describes a not-yet-recruiting Fast Track study evaluating exercise combined with weight loss medication versus weight loss medication alone in adults with overweight or obesity. The study will assess effects on body weight, body fat, blood sugar, and cholesterol. [Source 40]
https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07298915

**FDA Weight Reduction Guidance Replaces 2007 Draft**
FDA states that its January 2025 draft guidance on obesity and overweight revises and replaces the February 2007 draft guidance for industry on weight management. The guidance is intended for drugs and biological products regulated by CDER for reduction and long-term maintenance of body weight in patients with obesity or overweight. [Source 3]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/obesity-and-overweight-developing-drugs-and-biological-products-weight-reduction

**EMA Weight Control Guideline Addresses Adult Obesity Drug Development**
EMA’s guideline on medicinal products used in weight control provides guidance for clinical evaluation of new medicines intended to promote weight loss in obese adult patients. It also references an addendum for obesity treatment in children. [Source 13]
https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/clinical-evaluation-medicinal-products-used-weight-control-scientific-guideline

## Pain Related Conditions (Pain, Migraine, Gout)
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Updates Migraine Development Guidance for Acute Treatment**
FDA’s migraine guidance addresses the clinical development program and trial designs needed to support approval of prescription drugs for the acute treatment of migraine. The guidance does not apply to OTC products and does not address preventive treatment, which FDA says will be covered separately. [Source 9]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/migraine-developing-drugs-for-acute-treatment

**EMA Issues First Scientific Guideline for Gout Development**
EMA’s gout guideline provides general guidance on the development of medicinal products for gout, including urate-lowering therapy and anti-inflammatory treatment options. The document states that study design, inclusion criteria, primary endpoints, and trial duration depend on the treatment goal and mode of action. [Source 1]
https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/clinical-investigation-medicinal-products-treatment-gout-scientific-guideline

## Anti-Coagulation And Blood Disorders
### Guidelines & Updates
**ASH VTE Guidance Reaffirms Optimal Management of Anticoagulation Therapy**
ASH’s VTE guidance states that anticoagulant therapy is complex and should be managed after the anticoagulant choice has already been made. The guidance also notes that aggressive reversal should be reserved for life-threatening bleeding due to costs and thromboembolic risk. [Source 28][Source 29]
https://www.hematology.org/-/media/hematology/files/education/clinicians/guidelines-quality/documents/watermarked-pocket-guides/watermark-ash-vte-anticoagulation-pocket-guide.pdf
https://www.hematology.org/education/clinicians/guidelines-and-quality-care/clinical-practice-guidelines/venous-thromboembolism-guidelines/anticoagulation-therapy

**FDA Testosterone Review Cites Prior Cardiovascular Safety Trial in Men With Hypogonadism**
FDA’s testosterone product action references the TRAVERSE trial, which was conducted after the agency’s 2014 advisory committee meeting and industry-wide safety concerns regarding stroke, heart attack, and death in men using testosterone products. FDA states the TRAVERSE results did not show an increase in adverse cardiovascular outcomes in men using testosterone for hypogonadism. [Source 15]
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-alerts-and-statements/fda-issues-class-wide-labeling-changes-testosterone-products
