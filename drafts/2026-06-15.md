## Renal and Liver Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Guidance on Human Gene Therapy for Retinal Disorders Highlights Pediatric Development Considerations**
FDA’s guidance on human gene therapy for retinal disorders states that, where safety data support further clinical development, sponsors may consider a broader patient population in future trials. The guidance also emphasizes that many retinal disorders affect both children and adults, and that adult studies should be conducted prior to pediatric studies whenever feasible, with pediatric investigations subject to the safeguards in 21 CFR Part 50, Subpart D. [Source 1]
https://www.fda.gov/media/124641/download

**NASH Regulatory Review Continues to Emphasize Endpoint Uncertainty**
A review article on NASH regulatory considerations states that there are still no U.S. FDA-approved drugs or biological treatments for NASH or related liver diseases. It also notes that the regulatory pathway to approval remains unclear, despite official agency guidance, and identifies the FDA’s Division of Hepatology and Nutrition as the division that reviews IND applications and marketing applications for NAFLD/NASH-related products. [Source 7]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9061714

## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Finalizes Guidance for Nicotine Replacement Therapy Development**
The FDA finalized “Smoking Cessation and Related Indications: Developing Nicotine Replacement Therapy Drug Products; Guidance for Industry,” which is intended to assist sponsors in the clinical development of nicotine replacement therapy drug products for smoking cessation and related chronic indications. The guidance supersedes the February 22, 2019 draft. [Source 2]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/smoking-cessation-and-related-indications-developing-nicotine-replacement-therapy-drug-products

**Antimicrobial Resistance Policy Literature Calls for Clearer Trial Pathways**
A policy review on antimicrobial resistance argues that clear and feasible regulatory guidelines on clinical trial designs are needed to enable approval of new antibiotics and other antimicrobials. It further states that regulatory guidance is needed to support organism-specific antibiotic clinical trials and new approval pathways focused on medical need. [Source 8]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3738230

A review of pediatric obesity trial representation states that FDA guidance for weight-management drug products recommends inclusion of participants across a wide range of BMIs and evaluation of pharmacokinetics in that population. It also notes that the guidance emphasizes metabolic biomarkers such as hemoglobin A1C, blood pressure, and lipids, and recommends assessment for genetic or endocrinologic causes of obesity in pediatric patients. ## Male and Female Health
### Guidelines & Updates
**Contraception Recommendations Differ Across Regulatory Frameworks**
A review comparing regulatory recommendations states that major guidances agree on the need for and duration of contraception for genotoxic molecules in men and women, but that EMA/CHMP recommendations apply to both clinical trials and labeling, while the CTFG guidance includes contraception methods and pregnancy testing recommendations for clinical trials. The same source notes that the FDA 2019 guidance on reproductive toxicity testing and labeling for oncology products applies to oncology pharmaceuticals only. [Source 19]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10083981

FDA’s Office of Oncologic Diseases submission guidance states that pre-IND meeting requests or INDs for multiple solid tumors or hematologic malignancies are assigned based on whether a specific indication has been identified. The guidance also specifies how sponsors should identify such submissions in the cover letter subject line. [Source 10]

**Oncology Drug Development Review Notes Special Program Use and Development Timelines**
A review of precision oncology drug development states that oncology drugs have historically had greater access to FDA special programs than non-oncology drugs and that median approval time from NDA/BLA submission to final approval was shorter for oncology drugs. The source also notes that oncology development remains characterized by high attrition and regulatory burden. [Source 37]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5288167

An American Psychiatric Association practice guide states that, historically, pharmaceutical manufacturers have had to conduct eight clinical trials to obtain two that meet the FDA’s minimum threshold for approval of a medication. The guide also notes that antidepressant trials often use highly selected “pure patients,” which can affect the interpretability of trial data. [Source 32]

## # Guidelines & Updates
**Neurology Literature Continues to Support Biomarker-Driven Trial Design**
A neurology review states that CAMD developed AD and PD data standards with CDISC and achieved the first biomarker qualification from EMA for MRI. The same source notes that FDA draft guidance for early-stage Alzheimer’s assessment and EMA’s concept paper on revision of Alzheimer’s guidance were part of the broader regulatory context. [Source 15]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4322066

**Platform Trial Commentary Highlights Regulatory Friction in Neurodegeneration**
A recent commentary argues that multi-arm multi-stage platform trials are a credible path forward for Alzheimer’s disease, Parkinson’s disease, and motor neuron disease, and notes that the 2025 Alzheimer’s disease drug development pipeline includes 182 clinical trials evaluating 138 candidate treatments. It also states that platform trials require continuous biomarker infrastructure such as amyloid PET capacity, certified CSF collection, and NfL assay access. [Source 30]
https://www.clinicaltrialvanguard.com/article/intel-brief/platform-trials-are-neurologys-best-bet-so-why-is-the-fda-still-treating-them-like-experiments
