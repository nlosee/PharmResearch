## Infectious Disease
### Clinical Trials
**RSV Development Continues to Emphasize Endpoint Harmonisation and Pediatric Risk Controls**
A recent review notes that regulators prioritize clinically meaningful RSV endpoints such as RSV-confirmed MA-LRTI, hospitalization, or disease progression, with the EMA placing particular emphasis on standardized RSV-confirmed MA-LRTI definitions. The same review states that FDA and EMA both support early dialogue on endpoint selection, and that the FDA placed a hold on RSV vaccine trials in infants under two years of age or RSV-naive children aged 2–5 years following reports of increased severe illness in a pediatric trial. [Source 24][Source 31]
https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/microbiology/articles/10.3389/fmicb.2026.1745808/full

**Liposomal Amphotericin B Enters a Breakthrough Therapy-Designated Study in Solid Organ Transplant Recipients**
A ClinicalTrials.gov record describes a not-yet-recruiting, real-world observational study of liposomal amphotericin B in solid organ transplant recipients with invasive fungal disease. The study is designated Breakthrough Therapy and is framed around the high morbidity and mortality of invasive fungal disease in the setting of long-term immunosuppressive therapy. [Source 14]
https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07656493
