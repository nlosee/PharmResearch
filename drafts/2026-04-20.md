# Pharma Regulatory Pulse — 2026-04-20

## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Issues Draft Guidance on Development of Non-Opioid Analgesics for Chronic Pain**
FDA announced the availability of a draft guidance for industry, “Development of Non-Opioid Analgesics for Chronic Pain,” intended to assist sponsors in the development of non-opioid analgesics for the treatment of chronic pain. The document describes FDA’s current recommendations regarding phase 3 trials for prescription non-opioid analgesic products being developed to treat chronic pain. The guidance also responds to the SUPPORT Act, which directs FDA to issue or update existing guidance to help address challenges to developing non-opioid medical products to treat pain. [Source 1][Source 3]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/development-non-opioid-analgesics-chronic-pain

## Cardiovascular Conditions
### Clinical Trials
**FDA Class-Wide Testosterone Labeling Changes Follow TRAVERSE and ABPM Data**
FDA informed sponsors of testosterone products about class-wide labeling changes after review of TRAVERSE and required postmarket ambulatory blood pressure monitoring studies. FDA stated the ABPM studies confirmed an increase in blood pressure with use of all testosterone products, class-wide. [Source 15]
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-safety-and-availability/fda-issues-class-wide-labeling-changes-testosterone-products

**ACC Updates High Blood Pressure Clinical Guidance**
The ACC’s updated high blood pressure guidance reiterates BP treatment goals and notes a new approach to 10-year risk calculation using the PREVENT equation instead of the Pooled Cohort Equation. The update also states that elevated BP is linked to future adverse cardiac outcomes, including heart failure and stroke. [Source 17]
https://www.acc.org/latest-in-cardiology/articles/2025/10/01/01/new-in-clinical-guidance-hbp

## Endocrine Conditions
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Nonclinical Guidance Addresses Endocrine-Related Drug Toxicity**
FDA’s nonclinical evaluation guidance describes endocrine-related toxicities affecting sex hormones, hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal hormones, thyroid hormone, and related feedback pathways. The document emphasizes repeat-dose toxicity studies and evaluation of endocrine-sensitive organs, including thyroid, adrenal, reproductive organs, and pituitary. [Source 7]

**FDA Issues Class-Wide Testosterone Labeling Changes**
FDA’s testosterone labeling action is directly relevant to endocrine development programs, as the agency is requiring class-wide labeling changes based on TRAVERSE and postmarket ABPM findings. FDA states that the completed ABPM studies confirmed increased blood pressure with all testosterone products. [Source 15]
https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-safety-and-availability/fda-issues-class-wide-labeling-changes-testosterone-products

## Pain Related Conditions (Pain, Migraine, Gout)
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Issues Draft Guidance on Development of Non-Opioid Analgesics for Chronic Pain**
FDA announced draft guidance for industry on development of non-opioid analgesics for chronic pain, including recommendations on phase 3 trials. The guidance also responds to the SUPPORT Act and addresses how sponsors may demonstrate reduction in opioid use. [Source 1][Source 3]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/development-non-opioid-analgesics-chronic-pain

**RAPS Summarizes FDA Draft Guidance on Non-Opioid Pain Medications**
RAPS reports that FDA’s draft guidance addresses trial design, participant enrollment criteria, and evidence needed to show a pain medication helps reduce opioid use. The article also notes FDA’s willingness, in certain circumstances, to consider a single well-controlled trial with confirmatory evidence. [Source 3]
https://www.raps.org/resource/fda-issues-guidance-on-non-opioid-drug-development.html

**CDC Discontinues Updating Opioid NDC and Oral MME Conversion File**
CDC stated it is discontinuing updates to the opioid NDC and oral MME conversion file, and that the 2022 Clinical Practice Guideline provides CDC’s sole resource related to MME conversion factors. The source cautions that MME conversion factors should not be used to determine opioid conversion doses. [Source 5]
https://archive.cdc.gov/www_cdc_gov/opioids/data-resources/index.html

## Gastrointestinal Conditions
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Guidance on IBS Clinical Evaluation Focuses on Patient-Reported Symptoms**
FDA’s guidance for IBS drug development states that diagnosis and assessment of clinical status depend mainly on patient-reported signs and symptoms. The guidance emphasizes the challenge of reliably capturing clinically important treatment benefit in IBS trials. [Source 4]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/irritable-bowel-syndrome-clinical-evaluation-products-treatment

**ACG IBD Preventive Care Guidance Highlights Vaccinations and Screening**
The ACG IBD preventive care guidance summary includes vaccination assessment, cervical cancer screening, melanoma and non-melanoma skin cancer screening, osteoporosis, depression and anxiety, and smoking cessation. It also states that coordination between primary care and gastroenterology is necessary for IBD preventive care. [Source 25]
https://acgcdn.gi.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/ACG-Inflammatory-Bowel-Disease-Preventive-Care-Guideline-Summary.pdf

**Expert Review Compares FDA and EMA Ulcerative Colitis Development Guidance**
An expert panel review notes ongoing divergence and the need for harmonization in IBD clinical trial recommendations, including combination strategies and biomarker-supported personalized treatment approaches. The source suggests joint workshops or roundtables between regulators as a mechanism for convergence. [Source 9]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12280173/

**FDA Approves Caplacizumab for Pediatric aTTP**
FDA approved Cablivi (caplacizumab-yhdp) for injection for pediatric patients 12 years and older with acquired thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura in combination with plasma exchange and immunosuppressive therapy. FDA notes the bleeding risk is increased in patients taking antiplatelet agents or anticoagulants. [Source 14]
