## Renal and Liver Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Guidance on Patients With Organ Dysfunction Reiterates Renal and Hepatic Eligibility Considerations**
FDA guidance on patients with organ dysfunction states that when PK and major elimination pathways are not well understood, enrollment may be limited to patients with relatively preserved renal and hepatic function. The document recommends revising protocols as toxicity, PK, and/or PD data emerge to allow inclusion of patients with compromised organ function where safe dosage adjustments have been determined. [Source 5]
https://www.fda.gov/media/123745/download

**FDA Drug-Induced Liver Injury Guidance Addresses Hy’s Law Case Reporting and Stable Liver Disease Enrollment**
FDA guidance on drug-induced liver injury specifies that NDA and BLA submissions should include a listing of possible Hy’s Law cases by treatment group, with narrative summaries for each case. The same guidance notes that patients with stable underlying liver disease can be included cautiously in late-stage trials, but patients with acute viral, autoimmune, alcoholic, or other unstable hepatitis are generally not appropriate for trials other than those treating the acute illness. [Source 9]
https://www.fda.gov/media/116737/download

**KDIGO Hepatitis C in CKD Guideline Recommends Regimen Selection Based on GFR, Hepatic Fibrosis, and Transplant Considerations**
KDIGO’s hepatitis C in CKD guideline recommends selecting a regimen based on prior treatment history, drug–drug interactions, GFR, stage of hepatic fibrosis, kidney and liver transplant candidacy, and comorbidities. It also recommends pre-treatment assessment for drug–drug interactions between DAA-based regimens and concomitant medications, including immunosuppressive drugs in kidney transplant recipients. [Source 14]
https://kdigo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/KDIGO-2022-Hepatitis-C-in-CKD-Guideline.pdf

## Infectious Disease
### Clinical Trials
**FDA RSV Antiviral Guidance Outlines Development Program for Prophylaxis and Treatment**
FDA’s RSV guidance addresses clinical development of drugs and biological products for treatment and prevention of disease caused by RSV infection, with primary focus on antiviral products for RSV-related illness in infants and young children. The guidance also states that it covers nonclinical development for both prophylaxis and treatment. [Source 1]

## Cardiovascular Conditions
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Cardiovascular Endpoints Guidance Lists Primary Endpoints for Heart Failure, AF, and Pulmonary Hypertension**
FDA’s cardiovascular endpoints guidance identifies cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure or urgent heart failure visit as primary endpoints for heart failure trials. It also lists stroke and systemic embolism for nonvalvular atrial fibrillation and time to first adjudicated clinical worsening event for pulmonary hypertension. [Source 6]
https://www.fda.gov/media/167530/download

**AHA Commentary Reiterates FDA Expectation for Outcome or Valid Symptom Measures**
An AHA commentary notes that FDA guidance documents require efficacy in terms of outcomes with concomitant safety, or a favorable effect on a valid symptom measure with sufficient assurance of no adverse effect on outcomes. The commentary frames this in the context of cardiovascular drug and device development. [Source 39]
https://professional.heart.org/en/science-news/state-of-the-science-the-relevance-of-symptoms-in-cardiovascular-disease-and-research/commentary

## Male and Female Health
### Clinical Trials
**FDA Reproductive Risk Guidance Details Contraception and Pregnancy Testing Expectations**
FDA’s reproductive risk guidance states that protocol teams should justify any contraception requirement, periodically reassess reproductive potential during the study, and use barrier methods only in combination with another highly effective method for products with high fetal and reproductive risk. The guidance also addresses pregnancy testing, pregnancy registries, and informed consent. [Source 2]
https://rsc.niaid.nih.gov/sites/default/files/trpguidancereproriskfinal.pdf

**FDA Opens Docket on Menopause Hormone Therapy Risks and Benefits**
FDA opened a docket for public comments on menopause hormone therapy, including data that could support labeling updates. The agency said it is seeking perspectives on risks and benefits related to breast cancer, cardiovascular disease, genitourinary systems, bone health, and dementia, as well as timing of initiation, formulation, dose, and route of administration. [Source 10]
https://www.fda.gov/patients/fda-expert-panels/fda-expert-panel-menopause-and-hormone-replacement-therapy-women-07172025

**FDA Pediatric Rare Diseases Guidance Addresses Trial Design and Safety Monitoring**
FDA’s pediatric rare diseases guidance states that external independent data safety monitoring boards should be used during trials and that early stopping of a treatment arm for clinical decline should be considered. ## Pain Related Conditions (Pain, Migraine, Gout)
### Clinical Trials
**FDA Issues Draft Guidance for Non-Opioid Analgesics in Acute Pain**
FDA’s draft guidance for non-opioid analgesics for acute pain addresses trial design, outcome measures, safety considerations, and labeling claims related to opioid replacement or reduction. The guidance states that sponsors seeking such labeling should have early and regular discussions with FDA and may be eligible for expedited review programs where applicable. [Source 3]
https://www.fda.gov/media/156063/download

**FDA Draft Guidance for Non-Opioid Analgesics in Chronic Pain Recommends Placebo-Controlled Designs**
FDA’s chronic pain guidance recommends a concurrent placebo arm for effectiveness demonstrations when noninferiority margins cannot be reliably predefined. The guidance also states that robust results from at least one adequate and well-controlled trial are required to support labeling claims for reduction in opioid use. [Source 7]
https://www.fda.gov/media/188612/download

**CDC Opioid Prescribing Guidance Reiterates Nonopioid Therapies for Acute and Chronic Pain**
CDC’s 2022 opioid prescribing guideline states that nonopioid therapies are at least as effective as opioids for many common types of acute pain and are preferred for subacute and chronic pain. The guidance also recommends immediate-release opioids rather than extended-release and long-acting opioids when opioid therapy is initiated. [Source 24]
https://www.cdc.gov/overdose-prevention/hcp/clinical-guidance/recommendations-and-principles.html

## Oncology
### Guidelines & Updates
**NCCN Describes Real-Time Guideline Updates and Interim Panel Review Process**
NCCN states that its oncology guidelines incorporate real-time updates and that panels evaluate new information throughout the year, including new FDA approvals and expanded indications. The organization says interim updates may be published between annual updates when new data warrant changes. [Source 26]
https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/guidelines-process/development-and-update-of-guidelines

**FDA Oncology Endpoints Guidance Remains the Core Reference for NDA, BLA, and Supplemental Submissions**
FDA’s oncology endpoints guidance states that it applies to drugs for patients with existing cancer and supports effectiveness claims in NDAs, BLAs, and supplemental applications. The guidance also notes that it replaces the May 2007 version and references minimal residual disease considerations for hematologic malignancies. [Source 4]
https://www.fda.gov/media/71195/download

**FDA Guidance on Patients With Organ Dysfunction Supports Inclusion in Cancer Trials**
FDA’s guidance on patients with organ dysfunction states that eligibility criteria should use a contemporary, widely accepted equation to estimate GFR and that protocols should be revised to include patients with compromised organ function when safe dosage adjustments have been determined. The guidance also addresses cardiac and hepatic function and prior or concurrent malignancies. [Source 5]
https://www.fda.gov/media/123745/download
