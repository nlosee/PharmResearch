# Pharma Regulatory Pulse — 2026-03-22

## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Finalizes RSV Antiviral Development Guidance for Prophylaxis and Treatment**
FDA issued guidance for industry on the clinical development of drugs and biological products for the treatment and prevention of disease caused by respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) infection. The guidance addresses overall development programs and clinical trial designs, and it specifically recommends evaluating both antiviral activity and clinical signs and symptoms in adults in early-phase trials. [Source 1][Source 2]
https://www.fda.gov/files/drugs/published/Respiratory-Syncytial-Virus-Infection--Developing-Antiviral-Drugs-for-Prophylaxis-and-Treatment-Guidance-for-Industry.pdf

## Renal and Liver Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**KDIGO Updates Hepatitis C in CKD Clinical Practice Guidance**
KDIGO’s 2022 hepatitis C in CKD guideline states that major advances in HCV therapy have made treatment of an increasing number of patients with CKD and HCV feasible irrespective of genotype or severity of liver disease. The document also notes advances in diagnostic testing in liver disease, including non-invasive evaluation of hepatic disease. [Source 36]
https://kdigo.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/KDIGO-2022-Hepatitis-C-in-CKD-Guideline.pdf

**FDA Guidance on Drug-Induced Liver Injury Remains Relevant for NDA/BLA Submissions**
FDA’s guidance on drug-induced liver injury states that NDA and BLA submissions should include a listing of possible Hy’s Law cases by treatment group, with narrative summaries for each case. The guidance also notes that patients with acute viral, autoimmune, alcoholic, or other types of hepatitis are generally not appropriate for clinical trials other than trials of treatments for their acute illness. [Source 9]
https://www.fda.gov/media/116737/download

**KDIGO Drug Dosing Report Recommends Measured GFR-Based Labeling**
KDIGO’s drug dosing report states that drug labels should indicate dosage recommendations based on measured GFR rather than a specific estimation equation. It also recommends further evaluation of the safety and efficacy of proposed dosage regimens in postmarketing studies in patient populations not sufficiently represented in premarketing studies. [Source 31]
https://kdigo.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/KDIGO-DrugDosingReportFinal.pdf

## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Draft RSV Antiviral Guidance Sets Development Expectations for Prophylaxis and Treatment**
FDA’s draft guidance for RSV infection describes the agency’s current thinking on clinical development programs and trial designs for drugs and biological products intended to treat or prevent RSV disease. The document specifically emphasizes early-phase evaluation of both antiviral activity and clinical signs and symptoms in adults, with proof-of-concept trial designs discussed for RSV challenge studies. [Source 1][Source 2]
https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/respiratory-syncytial-virus-infection-developing-antiviral-drugs-prophylaxis-and-treatment-guidance

**FDA Division of Antivirals Scope Includes Influenza, Hepatitis, RSV, HIV-1, and Adenovirus**
FDA states that the Division of Antivirals oversees development, review, and regulation of products intended to prevent or treat infections caused by viruses including influenza, hepatitis, RSV, HIV-1, and adenovirus. The page is current as of June 25, 2025. [Source 33]
https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/cder-offices-and-divisions/office-infectious-diseases-oid-division-antivirals-dav

## Special Populations (Weight Loss, Transplants, Pediatric)
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Weight-Reduction Guidance Sets Pediatric Development Expectations**
FDA’s draft guidance for weight-reduction drugs states that pediatric assessments are required in certain products under PREA unless an exception, waiver, or deferral applies. The guidance recommends pediatric study plans early in development and notes that separate trials or cohorts are generally recommended for adolescents and younger pediatric subjects. [Source 3]
https://www.fda.gov/media/71252/download

**FDA Pediatric Rare Diseases Guidance Proposes Multi-Arm, Multi-Company Trial Approach**
FDA’s pediatric rare diseases guidance discusses a controlled, multi-arm, multi-company clinical trial model using Gaucher disease as an example. The document is intended to facilitate drug development in pediatric rare diseases while minimizing placebo exposure. [Source 4]
https://www.fda.gov/media/109465/download

## Pain Related Conditions (Pain, Migraine, Gout)
### Guidelines & Updates
**CDC Opioid Prescribing Guideline Addresses Chronic Pain and Acute Episodic Migraine**
CDC’s opioid prescribing guideline is intended to assist clinicians in weighing benefits and risks of opioid pain medication for chronic pain and for painful acute conditions including acute episodic migraine, kidney stone pain, dental pain, and postoperative pain. The guideline also references nonopioid pharmacologic treatments and nonpharmacologic treatments in its evidence base. [Source 17][Source 26]
https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/71/rr/rr7103a1.htm

**FDA Draft Guidance on Non-Opioid Analgesics for Acute Pain Addresses Opioid-Sparing Claims**
FDA’s draft guidance states that the term “opioid-sparing” is unlikely to be sufficiently descriptive to be meaningful in labeling for non-opioid analgesic products for acute pain. The guidance also notes that sponsors should discuss safety database requirements with FDA early in development. [Source 5]
https://www.fda.gov/media/156063/download

**FDA Draft Guidance on Non-Opioid Analgesics for Chronic Pain Discusses Trial Design and Estimands**
FDA’s draft guidance for non-opioid analgesics for chronic pain discusses trial design, outcome measures, safety considerations, and the need to prespecify estimands and intercurrent event handling. The document also notes that robust results from at least one adequate and well-controlled trial are required to support labeling claims related to opioid reduction. [Source 7]
https://www.fda.gov/media/188612/download
