# Pharma Regulatory Pulse — 2026-04-13

## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Approves First Gene Therapies for Sickle Cell Disease**
The FDA approved the first gene therapies for sickle cell disease, Casgevy and Lyfgenia, both based on patients’ own blood stem cells, which are modified, and are given back as a one-time, single-dose infusion following myeloablative conditioning. Patients treated with both products will be followed in long-term studies to evaluate safety and effectiveness. [Source 8]
https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-approves-first-gene-therapies-treat-patients-sickle-cell-disease

## Male and Female Health
### Guidelines & Updates
**ACOG Menstrual Suppression Guidance References U.S. MEC for Contraceptive Safety**
ACOG states that the U.S. Medical Eligibility Criteria for Contraceptive Use should be applied when considering hormonal therapy for menstrual suppression, particularly in patients with comorbidities or conditions. The guidance also notes that the etonogestrel implant is FDA-approved for 3 years for contraception and may be continued up to 5 years for contraception, though use past 3 years may not be effective for menstrual suppression. **FDA-Referenced Pediatric IBD Guidance Emphasizes Endpoints and Alignment With Adult Programs**
A CenterWatch summary of FDA draft guidance on pediatric IBD states that pediatric Crohn’s disease studies may use co-primary endpoints of clinical remission and endoscopic remission, with endoscopic response potentially used as a primary endpoint in some cases. The source also notes that pediatric study design should generally align with the adult Phase III program’s design, population, endpoints, and timing of assessments. [Source 15]
