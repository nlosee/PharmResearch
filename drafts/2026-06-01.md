# Pharma Regulatory Pulse — 2026-06-01

## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Proposes Updated Draft Guidance for Anti-Obesity Drug Development**
The FDA published draft guidance updating its 2007 recommendations for drugs and biologics for weight reduction in patients with obesity, with emphasis on BMI-based enrollment, chronic weight management, and sustained weight loss/maintenance for at least 1 year. The draft also expands discussion of adult and pediatric trial populations, phase 1–3 design considerations, efficacy endpoints, safety evaluation, and statistical principles, including combination use and pediatric assessment. [Source 1][Source 7]
https://www.raps.org/resource/fda-proposes-update-to-18-year-guidance-on-develop.html

## Renal and Liver Disease

The BLA is supported by the Phase 3 ConfIdeS trial, which evaluated imlifidase in highly sensitized kidney transplant adults with positive crossmatch against a deceased donor. The trial’s primary endpoint was mean estimated glomerular filtration rate at 12 months. [Source 6]

## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Office of Infectious Diseases Structure and Scope**
FDA’s Office of Infectious Diseases oversees review and regulation of applications for drugs to prevent or treat infectious diseases, including products for bacterial, fungal, parasitic, and viral infections. The office comprises the Division of Anti-Infectives, Division of Antivirals, and Division of Pharmacology/Toxicology for Infectious Diseases. [Source 21]
https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/cder-offices-and-divisions/office-infectious-diseases-oid

**IDSA Calls for Clearer FDA Antimicrobial Development Guidance**
IDSA policy materials call for clearer FDA regulatory guidance on clinical trial designs for antibiotics and other antimicrobials, including organism-specific trials, superiority trials, and historically controlled studies. The source also states that current non-inferiority margin approaches should not further discount conservative efficacy estimates. [Source 23]
https://www.idsociety.org/globalassets/idsa/policy--advocacy/current_topics_and_issues/antimicrobial_resistance/agriculture/statements-manually-added/040711-combating-antimicrobial-resistance-policy-recommendations-to-save-lives.pdf

## Pain Related Conditions (Pain, Migraine, Gout)
### Guidelines & Updates
**International Headache Society Updates Controlled Trial Endpoints for Acute Migraine**
The guideline states that, for regulatory purposes, FDA recommends co-primary endpoints of pain freedom at 2 hours and absence of the most bothersome associated symptom at 2 hours in acute migraine trials. It also discusses early-intervention trials and the need for clear instructions when subjects are asked to treat after pain becomes moderate or severe. [Source 34]
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6501455

**FDA Clears NSAID/Triptan Combination for Acute Migraine in Adults**
The source reports FDA clearance of a rizatriptan/meloxicam combination for adults with migraine. Approval was based on Phase 3 MOMENTUM, INTERCEPT, and MOVEMENT studies, with more than 21,000 migraine attacks treated across the development program. [Source 28]
https://www.medscape.com/viewarticle/fda-clears-nsaid-triptan-combo-migraine-adults-2025a10002j1

**FDA Comment Materials on Non-Opioid Analgesics for Chronic Pain**
Public comments submitted to FDA argue that chronic-pain trials should not initiate or maintain long-term opioid therapy and should avoid enriched enrollment randomized withdrawal designs. The submission also recommends broader, more representative trial populations and hybrid or decentralized trial designs. [Source 9]
https://www.center4research.org/fda-public-comment-nonopioid-analgesic-research
