## Regulatory Spotlight
**FDA Expedited Programs Continue to Shape Development Strategy Across Multiple Therapeutic Areas**

## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**GINA Notes Regulatory Approval as a Trigger for Asthma Therapy Recommendations**
GINA states that for new asthma therapies, recommendations are generally made after approval by at least one major regulatory agency, such as the FDA or EMA, because regulators often have more safety and efficacy data than are available in the peer-reviewed literature. GINA also notes that some recommendations may be off-label in certain countries due to differences in regulatory approvals. [Source 6][Source 10]
https://ginasthma.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/GINA-2025_tracked-for-archive-WMSA.pdf

## Cardiovascular Conditions
### Clinical Trials
**Pulmonary Hypertension and Hypertension Programs Continue to Anchor Cardiovascular Development**
A ClinicalTrials.gov record describes an expanded access framework in the context of pulmonary hypertension associated right heart failure, while FDA’s antihypertensive development guidance continues to emphasize assessment of hypotension, cardiac rhythm or conduction effects, coronary steal, and target organ damage. The guidance also states that patients with concomitant illness such as diabetes mellitus and coronary heart disease should generally be included in antihypertensive studies. [Source 5][Source 6]
