## Infectious Disease
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Draft RSV Antiviral Guidance Outlines Development Program and Early-Phase Efficacy Considerations**
The FDA draft guidance addresses clinical development of drugs and biological products for treatment and prevention of RSV infection. It recommends evaluating both antiviral activity and clinical signs and symptoms in adults in early-phase trials and discusses proof-of-concept trial designs, including randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled studies in experimentally infected healthy adults. [Source 5]
https://www.fda.gov/files/drugs/published/Respiratory-Syncytial-Virus-Infection--Developing-Antiviral-Drugs-for-Prophylaxis-and-Treatment-Guidance-for-Industry.pdf

## Anti-Coagulation And Blood Disorders
### Guidelines & Updates
**FDA Clinical Review for LYFGENIA Describes Sickle Cell Disease Pathophysiology and Hemolytic Anemia**
The FDA clinical review memo describes sickle cell disease as a hereditary blood disorder caused by a β-globin mutation leading to HbS polymerization, red blood cell sickling, chronic hemolytic anemia, and vaso-occlusive events. The memo also notes that chronic hemolysis is a hallmark finding and identifies laboratory markers including indirect bilirubin, reticulocyte count, lactate dehydrogenase, and haptoglobin. [Source 10]
https://www.fda.gov/media/179352/download

**FDA Review of Voxelotor Program References Stroke Prevention Trial Design in Sickle Cell Disease**
The FDA review memo states that the applicant proposed a controlled study to confirm clinical benefit by evaluating voxelotor’s effect on stroke risk reduction as measured by transcranial doppler flow velocity in pediatric patients with sickle cell anemia. It also notes that the postmarketing requirement called for completion of a Phase 3 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in pediatric patients with sickle cell disease. [Source 20]
https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/nda/2019/213137Orig1s000Multidiscipline.pdf

**FDA Clinical Review for CASGEVY Highlights Limitations of Uncontrolled SCD Data**
The FDA review for exa-cel states that patient-reported quality-of-life data were not evaluated for USPI purposes because of limitations in uncontrolled, open-label trials. It also describes sickle cell disease as a group of hemoglobinopathies characterized by hemolytic anemia, vaso-occlusive crises, and chronic pain. [Source 25]
https://www.fda.gov/media/175236/download
